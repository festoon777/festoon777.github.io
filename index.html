      Step By Step Guidance

    Since having a baby will change everything in your life, Family Birth Center at Menifee Hospital is prepared to guide you trough the entire journey and a little further. Natural births are now mainly sought after, and we offer various options for births. These include birthing suites–a private room with a bath. This allows for labor, delivery, and recovery in the same room. Rest assured that both you and the baby are closely monitored throughout the labor by our caring staff.

    Our private rooms provide a warm and comfortable setting for new mothers, their babies and visiting family.

    Even before the baby steps into this world, we can offer you helpful information regarding any concern you might have. Our caring and skilled staff will always be there to support you and answer your questions, allowing you to be prepared for the arrival of your new family member.

    We even have childbirth & maternity classes in which we make sure that you and your family are prepared for having a baby. You and the ones close to you will be assisted and advised on how to prepare for your newborn, what are the basics in baby care, how to breastfeed your baby and even infant CPR instructions.

  •   Maternity Classes and Tours

    This class is for expectant parents who have not previously taken a childbirth class. This six week class will give you the opportunity to experience:

    Increased retention of information and coping skills for labor.

    Decreased anxiety about pregnancy, labor and birth with six weeks of support from a birth professional. Support from other pregnant families.

    Bring 2 pillows to practice labor techniques with your partner. You may bring snacks. It is recommended that you begin this class in approximately the 28-30th week of your pregnancy.


    Topics include:
    • Challenges and joys of pregnancy
    • Labor process
    • Breathing, relaxation and labor support
    • Care of the newborn
    • Adjustment to your new role as parents
    • Tour of the Family Birth Center
    • Breastfeeding
    • Babyproofing (Home/Child Safety)
    • Diaper Duty for Dads (Find out the important role dads play in the pregnancy and after delivery)
    • Parenting (New Born Care and Group Support)
    • Support for Pre/Post-Partum Depression
    • Infant CPR

    To register for classes please contact xyz at maternity@MenifeeHospital.org or call 951-555-1212

  •   Packing for your hospital stay

    As you approach week 35 in your pregnancy, it's a good idea to be prepared by packing your bag for the hospital and putting it in the car. Most of what you bring will depend on your personal preferences and comforts, in addition to your identification, health insurance card and a car seat for baby's first trip home.

      Some Suggestions:

    • Personal identification and health insurance card
    • List of phone numbers and e-mail addresses for people you want to contact after the delivery
    • Lip balm or Chap Stick® (your lips get dry during labor)
    • A watch with a second hand to time contractions
    • Toothbrush and toothpaste
    • Barrettes or band to tie back long hair
    • Pillow from home with a pillowcase other than white (to distinguish your pillow from the hospital's)
    • Music or CDs
    • A going-home outfit for you and baby
    • Blanket for baby
    • A nursing gown and/or comfortable robe that opens all the way down the front
    • Nursing bras and nursing pads
    • Slippers/socks to keep your feet warm
    • Personal hygiene items you will want (shampoo, brush, etc.)
    • You will also need an infant car seat properly installed in your car before you are discharged from the Family Birth Center.
